Disney · The Walt Disney Company

Streamlining Price Change Operations

Redesigning internal tools used by pricing analysts across theme parks to reduce errors, increase transparency, and accelerate approvals for multi‑million‑dollar price updates.

Role
Lead Product Designer
Timeline
12 months
Team
PM, Tech Lead, 6 Engineers, Research

Overview

Disney’s revenue management team manages thousands of prices across theme parks, resorts, tickets, and add‑ons. Legacy tools made it difficult for analysts to understand the full impact of their changes, collaborate with partners, and move quickly during time‑sensitive pricing windows.

I partnered with product, engineering, and operations leaders to define a new web‑based workflow that centralizes requests, improves data visibility, and reduces the cognitive load of managing complex pricing rules.

Goals

  • Reduce time from initial request to final approval for price changes.
  • Increase confidence in pricing decisions by surfacing relevant context and history.
  • Decrease operational errors caused by fragmented tools and manual handoffs.

Outcomes

  • 35% faster approval times for standard price changes.
  • Significant reduction in back‑and‑forth clarifying questions from partners.
  • Clear audit trail and single source of truth for in‑flight and historical changes.

Process

Understanding the current workflow

I began with field research and contextual interviews with analysts, finance partners, and operations leads across parks. We mapped the end‑to‑end process from request intake through approvals and deployment, documenting tools, workarounds, and pain points.

Defining scenarios and requirements

From research, we distilled key scenarios such as “minor price update,” “new product launch,” and “emergency change.” Each scenario surfaced different data needs, participants, and constraints, which shaped our information architecture and permission model.

Iterating on workflows and UI

I translated requirements into flow diagrams, low‑fidelity wireframes, and interactive prototypes. We ran weekly reviews with cross‑functional stakeholders, incorporating feedback on terminology, edge cases, and alignment with downstream systems.

Partnering closely with engineering

Throughout implementation, I maintained detailed specs and worked side‑by‑side with engineers to refine interactions, validation states, and empty‑state content. We prioritized accessibility, keyboard navigation, and performance for data‑heavy tables.

Key experience highlights

Unified change request view

Analysts can now see all relevant context in one place: impacted products, historical pricing, approvals, and dependencies. Clear status indicators help teams understand where a request is blocked.

Guided workflows for complex scenarios

For high‑impact changes, the tool guides analysts through required checks and documentation, reducing missed steps and last‑minute surprises.

Transparent approvals

Stakeholders can review proposed changes with clear diffs, business rationale, and downstream impact, making it easier to give fast, confident approvals.

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of designing for operational resilience. By grounding decisions in real‑world constraints and building strong relationships with cross‑functional partners, we shipped a tool that not only improved efficiency but also built trust in the pricing process.
